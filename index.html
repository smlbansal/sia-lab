The Safe and Intelligent Autonomy Lab is part of the Department of Aeronautics and Astronautics at Stanford. We seek to design autonomous systems and robots that operate with guaranteed safety and performance in new and uncertain environments. The current focus of the lab is to ensure safety when these systems leverage machine learning and AI for decision-making and control.

To achieve this, our research emphasizes developing fundamental frameworks and computational tools that integrate safety into every stage of the learning process. This includes methods to provide safety assurances during the design/training phase (e.g., via safe learning and correct-by-construction policies); continuous safety monitoring during operation (e.g., through uncertainty quantification and anomaly detection); and dynamically adapting to new safety risks during the systemâ€™s lifetime (e.g., via developing new safety and reachability methods for nonlinear and high-dimensional system). Algorithmically, our research blends classical control theory and modern machine learning methods towards this goal, with applications across robotics, surveillance, air traffic management, space exploration, and autonomous driving.
